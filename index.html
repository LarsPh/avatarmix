Abstract

Existing 3D avatar outfit transfer methods often face a trade-off between outfit fidelity, identity preservation, and geometric robustness. Methods that lift 2D edits into 3D can suffer from texture degradation, hallucinated details, and multi-view inconsistency, while layered body-and-clothing methods can introduce garment-body intersections and require complex collision handling.

AvatarMix takes a different approach. We directly compose the head and clothed body from two high-fidelity Gaussian avatars: a User avatar that provides identity cues and a Model avatar that provides the desired outfit. This explicit 3D composition preserves the geometry and texture of the source outfit while avoiding layer-intersection issues by design.

To make cross-avatar composition practical, AvatarMix introduces GSReshape, a mesh-based Gaussian retargeting module that reshapes the model's clothed body to match the user's physique. We further introduce a two-tier diffusion refinement strategy: SeamFix locally repairs hair and neck seams, while FullbodyFix optionally restores garment appearance when body reshaping introduces visible artifacts. Both refiners operate on renders from an already 3D-consistent Gaussian avatar, limiting multi-view artifacts compared to conventional 2D-to-3D editing pipelines.

Task & Motivation

Task

Given two mesh-based 3D Gaussian avatars, combine the User's identity with the Model's outfit. Identity is treated beyond the face: it includes the head and neck, body shape and scale, and skin tone.

Why is this hard?

  • 2D-to-3D try-on can degrade garment texture and view consistency.
  • Layered garment transfer can introduce body-clothing intersections.
  • Naive composition creates head-neck seams and segmentation artifacts.
  • Body reshaping should preserve detailed garment appearance.

Method Overview

Mesh-based Avatar Reconstruction

Reconstruct high-fidelity mesh-based 3DGS avatars from multi-view images, with semantic segmentation and SMPL-X fitting for part-aware geometric operations.

Cross-Avatar Composition

Align the User's head and neck to the Model pose, combine them with the Model's clothed body, and transfer the User's skin tone to preserve identity consistency.

GSReshape

Retarget the Model's clothed body to the User's physique by jointly deforming the mesh and its attached Gaussians, enabling body-shape-aware outfit personalization.

SeamFix & FullbodyFix

Repair seam and reshaping artifacts with diffusion-based refiners, then use refined multi-view renders to update the composed 3DGS avatar.

Training Artifact Refiners

Cross-avatar compositions do not naturally provide pixel-aligned ground truth for training artifact refiners. AvatarMix synthesizes such pairs with a double-swapping strategy. A first head swap introduces realistic composition artifacts, and a second swap returns the avatar to the original layout, yielding artifact/ground-truth pairs aligned in image space.

SeamFix is trained on portrait crops focusing on the head and neck region, while FullbodyFix is trained on full-body renders to restore garment and skin artifacts. Both branches fine-tune LoRA adapters of a diffusion-based artifact refinement backbone.

Results Summary

AvatarMix is evaluated on THUman2.0 against representative 3D virtual try-on and 3DGS editing baselines. Compared with VTON360 and TIP-Editor, AvatarMix better preserves the target outfit, the user's head and neck identity, and multi-view consistency.

Key Takeaways

  • Compose first, repair locally.
  • Mesh-based Gaussians make geometric identity transfer practical.
  • Diffusion refinement acts as cleanup, not full outfit generation.
  • The framework preserves outfit details, facial identity, skin tone consistency, and multi-view coherence.

Limitations & Future Work

  • Requires high-quality mesh-based Gaussian avatars as input.
  • FullbodyFix is currently triggered by visual inspection.
  • Animatable cross-avatar outfit transfer remains future work.
